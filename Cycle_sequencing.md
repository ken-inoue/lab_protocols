## Cycle Sequencing

### Summary
This is a procedure for cycle sequencing. This protocol was designed specifically for Sanger sequencing. Each study will require fine tuning of the protocol as PCR protocols may not transfer well from one study to another. This protocol provides a starting point but may need modifications (see PCR optimization procedure and troubleshooting).

### Equipment & supplies
- Vortex
- Pipettes
- Pipette tips
- Microcentrifuge tubes
- PCR tubes
- Table-top spinner
- Thermal cycler

### Reagents & standards
- Cleaned PCR products (see [PCR (general & Sanger sequencing)](PCR.md) [PCR cleanup (EXOSAP)](PCR_exosap.md))
- Taq Master Mix (GoTaq Master Mix, Promega)
- Forward & reverse primers
- Molecular-grade H2O

### Procedure
To minimize pipette error and chances of contamination, you will first make a master mix solution depending on how many samples you are running plus 2 (one for a negative control and one to account for pipetting error). This master mix solution is then put into each tube so that you are not putting in each reagent into each tube separately.

1.	Thaw all reagents and keep them on ice. Briefly vortex and spin to collect the material at the bottom of the tubes. DO NOT vortex Bigdye.
2.	Make the following PCR reaction mix solutions on ice. Two master mixes are needed (one for each forward and reverse primers).

The following is an example for samples in a 10-µL reaction volume.

Master Mix #1 for 10-µL reaction volume:
|Reagents	|Amount| Final concentration|
|:--------|:-----|:-------------------|
|Bigdye	| µL| X|
|Terminator 5x Sequencing Buffer |  µL| X|
|Forward primer (10 µM)	| µL| 0.1 – 1.0 µM|
|Molecular grade H2O	| µL| N.A.|

Master Mix #2 for 10-µL reaction volume:
|Reagents	|Amount| Final concentration|
|:--------|:-----|:-------------------|
|Bigdye	| µL| X|
|Terminator 5x Sequencing Buffer |  µL| X|
|Reverse primer	(10 µM) | µL| 0.1 – 1.0 µM|
|Molecular grade H2O	| µL| N.A.|

Added individually to each well:
|Reagents	|Amount| Final concentration| 
|:--------|:-----|:-------------------|
|DNA template (10 ng/µL) |1 µL| <250 ng|

3. Add 9µL of master mix to each well of a new PCR plate. In the first 48 wells (columns 1-6) of the plate, add the master mix containing the forward primer. In the remaining 48 wells (columns 7-12), add the master mix containing the reverse primer. Cover the plate, spin down, and move to the post-PCR area to add cleaned PCR products.
4. Thaw and spin the plate of cleaned PCR products (see [PCR cleanup (EXOSAP)](PCR_exosap.md)).
5. Using a multichannel pipette, aliquot 1µL of cleaned PCR products into the wells containing the master mix. Each sample will be used in two wells (one well containing the forward primer master mix, one well containing the reverse primer master mix. Eg, sample #1 will be aliquoted into wells A1 and A7, sample #2 will be aliquoted into wells B1 and B7, and so on.) Use a new tip for each sample. Run one sample with DNA but only master mix. Label this tube as X or NC for negative control. (If you get amplification in the negative control, something has been contaminated).
6. Remove any bubbles in the tubes by spinning in the fish-eye centrifuge. If this does not work, try popping bubbles with a small pipette tip.
7. Close tubes and place them into the thermal cycler and run program. ('Bigdye' program).
8. Move immediately to [Sanger sequencing](Sanger_Sequencing.md). If this is not possible, samples can be stored short-term at -20°C.
